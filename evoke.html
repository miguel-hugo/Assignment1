EVOKE Lab Website

Website project for social justice and data lab based in UC Irvine

Website Background

As an undergraduate research assistant, I was initially brought onto the team with two responsibilities: undergoing research on the usage and effects of data dashboards and creating/maintaining the lab website, the latter of which is the focus of this page.

Throughout my time as an assistant, I familiarized myself with WordPress to single handedly create the site and organize the content of the lab. Furthermore, I also took charge in redesigning the visual aspects of the lab, including the logo and theme colors. Below are some of the artifacts that went into creating the site.

NOTE: The current EVOKE website has been redesigned by the new research assistant.

Site Artifacts

Logo

One of the tasks I was responsible for was to redesign the lab logo. The supervisor wanted a more isometric logo compared to the more outdated logo. After sifting through numerous inspirations and creating multiple brainstorming sketches, I was able to come up with the logo you see displayed here using Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.

The house was created with an isometric perspective with an E marked on the wall to represent the Evoke name. Furthermore, the door is left open as a way to represent the inclusivity that the lab consistently promotes as a value. Overall, it serves as a simple, unique, and meaningful logo to represent the lab.
