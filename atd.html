Access the Data

UI/UX Designing for a community-driven project.

What is Access the Data?

Access the Data is a volunteer-based initiative that aims to expand the access of local government data to all communities as well as providing ways to teach individuals easier ways to understand and analyze these datasets and use them for their own benefit.

Access the Data is one project within the community of Hack for LA, a brigade of civic-minded tech volunteers dedicated to using their skills to develop tools for communities in need. Find out more about Access the Data and Hack for LA here.

In my time as a UI/UX Designer, I assisted in propelling the Design team to reach the first MVP and first usability testing period by helping in the creation of storyboards, sketches, mockups, and interactive prototypes. Furthermore, I also took part in helping the Research team conduct UX Practices, including comparative analyses and usability testing.

NOTE: All work referenced and displayed are not my property and are owned by the Access the Data team and Hack for LA.

Accessing the Artifacts

Storyboards

The storyboard sketches made here are meant to display how potential users can benefit from the tool we were in the early stages of creating. From my end, I envisioned two different stories that successfully displayed the struggle of users trying to find readable and accessible data to help them in their journeys to solve the issues they aim to solve within their community. By displaying these difficulties, the team is able to get a better sense of what audiences we need to design our tool for and how to better design our tool for them.
